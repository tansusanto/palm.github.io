Palm Oil

Palm oil is obtained the fruit of the oil palm tree. Palm oil is naturally red in color due to its high content of alpha and beta-carotenoids. It is a common cooking ingredient in tropical countries. Its use in the commercial food industry in other parts of the world is driven by its low production cost and its oxidative stability when used for frying.

Palm Kernel Shell (PKS)

Palm kernel shells (or PKS) are the shell fractions left after the nut has been removed after crushing in the Palm Oil mill. It is a fibrous material, brownish-yellow in color. Its particle sizes normally ranging between 5 mm and 40 mm with high calorific value, averaging 4000 Kcal/kg, and low ash content.
